Side Effects and Minimization

Common side effects caused by excipients and how to minimize them.

  • Sesame Oil (in injections): Hypersensitivity reactions. Minimize by using alternative oils like soybean oil and testing for allergies.
  • Lanolin (wool fat): Skin irritation. Minimize by substituting with synthetic alternatives or hypoallergenic options.
  • Lactose: Intolerance causing digestive issues. Minimize by using lactose-free formulations or alternatives like mannitol.
  • Sodium Starch Glycolate: Swelling and leakage. Minimize by controlling moisture and using in appropriate quantities.
  • General Dyes and Sugars: Allergic reactions or intolerances. Minimize by selecting excipients based on patient profiles and regulatory guidelines.

Source: Based on common pharmaceutical excipient data from reliable sources.
